# Glossary

This glossary defines specialized terms used throughout the SDG Decision Intelligence Framework.

## Notation

Mathematical notation used throughout the framework documentation:

- **G = (V, E)**: Graph representing SDG network, where V is the set of nodes (SDGs) and E is the set of edges (interactions)
- **V**: Set of SDGs (nodes), |V| = n = 17
- **E**: Set of interactions (edges), |E| = m = 136 pairwise relationships
- **W**: Edge weights representing synergy coefficients, W ∈ [-1, +1]
- **n**: Number of SDGs (nodes)
- **m**: Number of alternatives/initiatives
- **σ_st**: Number of shortest paths between nodes s and t
- **C(v)**: Centrality measure of node v
- **B(v)**: Betweenness centrality of node v
- **P(v)**: PageRank score of node v
- **I**: Impact Score
- **S**: Sustainability Score
- **F**: Feasibility Score
- **A**: SDG Alignment Score
- **O**: Overall Score
- **w_i**: Weight for criterion i
- **λ_ij**: Synergy coefficient between SDG i and SDG j

## Framework-Specific Terms

### SDG Synergy Coefficient

Numerical value in the interval [-1, +1] representing the strength and direction of interaction between two Sustainable Development Goals.

- **Positive values** (+1 to 0): Indicate reinforcing interactions where progress in one SDG accelerates progress in another
- **Negative values** (0 to -1): Indicate tradeoffs where progress in one SDG may impede progress in another
- **Zero**: Indicates neutral interaction with no significant relationship

Example: SDG 4 (Quality Education) and SDG 8 (Decent Work) have a positive synergy coefficient because education enables workforce productivity.

### SDG Influence Network

Graph representation of Sustainable Development Goals where nodes correspond to SDGs and weighted edges represent synergies or tradeoffs. This network model enables analysis of systemic effects, cascading influences, and network-based metrics.

The network is typically represented as G = (V, E) where:
- V = {SDG₁, SDG₂, ..., SDG₁₇} (17 nodes)
- E = {(SDG_i, SDG_j) | i ≠ j} (136 edges)
- Edge weights ∈ [-1, +1] representing synergy coefficients

### Synergy Balance Index

Aggregate measure describing the ratio of positive interactions to total interactions within a selected SDG portfolio or initiative.

**Formula**: SBI = (positive synergies) / (total interactions)

**Interpretation**:
- SBI > 0.5: Portfolio has more synergies than tradeoffs
- SBI = 0.5: Balanced portfolio
- SBI < 0.5: Portfolio has more tradeoffs than synergies

### Decision Intelligence

Interdisciplinary field combining data science, systems thinking, decision theory, and artificial intelligence to improve strategic decision-making. In this framework, decision intelligence refers to the systematic approach of transforming complex SDG interactions into actionable, explainable insights for portfolio design.

### Explainable Impact Modeling

Approach that decomposes impact scores into interpretable factors so decision-makers can understand the drivers behind recommendations. Each score is broken down into constituent factors (e.g., beneficiary efficiency, risk-adjusted return, synergy strength) with traceable mathematical formulas and evidence bases.

### Portfolio Optimization

Process of selecting a combination of initiatives that maximizes desired outcomes under resource constraints. In the SDG context, this involves selecting projects that maximize overall impact while respecting budget, timeline, and risk constraints, considering network effects and SDG interactions.

### Cascading Failure Path

Sequence of SDG interactions through which a failure or negative effect propagates through the network. For example, a project negatively impacting SDG 8 (Economic Growth) may cascade to affect SDG 13 (Climate Action) through the tradeoff relationship between these goals.

### Confidence Score

Measure of uncertainty quantification for framework outputs, typically derived from Monte Carlo simulation results. Confidence scores indicate the stability of rankings or scores under parameter variations, often expressed as confidence intervals or probability distributions.

### Executive Insight

High-level, actionable recommendation generated by the framework for decision-makers. Executive insights synthesize complex analytical results into clear guidance, often including confidence intervals, risk assessments, and specific action items.

### Decision Intelligence Layer

Architectural component that transforms raw analytical results into decision-relevant insights. This layer applies interpretation, context, and business logic to convert mathematical outputs into actionable recommendations.

## Universal Terms

## A

**AHP (Analytic Hierarchy Process)**: A structured technique for organizing and analyzing complex decisions, based on mathematics and psychology. Uses pairwise comparisons to derive ratio scales from discrete comparisons.

## B

**Betweenness Centrality**: A graph theory metric that measures the extent to which a node lies on the shortest paths between other nodes. High betweenness indicates a node acts as a bridge between different parts of the network.

**Borda Count**: A voting method used for consensus ranking across multiple MCDA methods. Each method assigns ranks, and these are aggregated to produce a final consensus ranking.

## C

**Centrality**: A family of graph metrics that identify the most important nodes in a network. Includes degree centrality, betweenness centrality, closeness centrality, and PageRank.

**Closeness Centrality**: A metric that measures how close a node is to all other nodes in the network. High closeness indicates a node can quickly reach or be reached by other nodes.

**Community Detection**: The process of identifying groups or clusters of nodes that are more densely connected to each other than to the rest of the network.

**Composite Indicator**: An aggregated index formed when individual indicators are compiled into a single index based on an underlying model.

## D

**Degree Centrality**: The simplest centrality metric, measuring the number of direct connections a node has.

## E

**ELECTRE**: A family of multi-criteria decision analysis methods based on outranking relations. Uses concordance and discordance analysis to compare alternatives.

**Emergence**: The phenomenon where complex patterns arise from relatively simple interactions between components. In this framework, emergent behaviors refer to unexpected outcomes from SDG interactions.

## L

**Label Propagation**: A community detection algorithm that assigns labels to nodes based on the labels of their neighbors, iteratively propagating labels through the network.

## M

**MCDA (Multi-Criteria Decision Analysis)**: A sub-discipline of operations research that explicitly considers multiple criteria in decision-making. Provides structured methods for evaluating alternatives based on conflicting objectives.

**Monte Carlo Simulation**: A computational algorithm that relies on repeated random sampling to obtain numerical results. Used in this framework for uncertainty quantification and robustness testing.

## P

**PageRank**: An algorithm used to measure the importance of nodes in a network based on the concept that important nodes are linked to by other important nodes. Originally developed for web search ranking.

**PROMETHEE**: A ranking method in multi-criteria decision analysis that uses preference functions to compare alternatives pairwise.

## R

**Resilience**: The capacity of a system to absorb disturbance and reorganize while undergoing change, so as to still retain essentially the same function, structure, identity, and feedbacks.

**Risk Propagation**: The process by which risks spread through a network, potentially causing cascading failures. In this framework, modeled through dependency graphs and betweenness analysis.

## S

**Synergy**: Combined effects that are greater than the sum of individual effects. In SDG context, positive synergies occur when progress in one goal accelerates progress in another.

**Systemic Risk**: Risk that affects an entire system rather than just individual components. Characterized by correlation, contagion, and cascading effects.

## T

**TOPSIS (Technique for Order Preference by Similarity to Ideal Solution)**: A multi-criteria decision analysis method that ranks alternatives based on their distance to an ideal solution and anti-ideal solution.

**Tradeoff**: A situation where gaining something requires losing something else. In SDG context, tradeoffs occur when progress in one goal impedes progress in another.

## U

**Uncertainty Quantification**: The process of quantifying uncertainty in model inputs and propagating it through to outputs, typically using statistical methods like Monte Carlo simulation.

## W

**Weight Sensitivity**: Analysis of how changes in the weights assigned to different criteria affect the final ranking or decision outcome. Used to assess robustness of MCDA results.
