# Glossary

This glossary defines specialized terms used throughout the SDG Decision Intelligence Framework.

## A

**AHP (Analytic Hierarchy Process)**: A structured technique for organizing and analyzing complex decisions, based on mathematics and psychology. Uses pairwise comparisons to derive ratio scales from discrete comparisons.

## B

**Betweenness Centrality**: A graph theory metric that measures the extent to which a node lies on the shortest paths between other nodes. High betweenness indicates a node acts as a bridge between different parts of the network.

**Borda Count**: A voting method used for consensus ranking across multiple MCDA methods. Each method assigns ranks, and these are aggregated to produce a final consensus ranking.

## C

**Centrality**: A family of graph metrics that identify the most important nodes in a network. Includes degree centrality, betweenness centrality, closeness centrality, and PageRank.

**Closeness Centrality**: A metric that measures how close a node is to all other nodes in the network. High closeness indicates a node can quickly reach or be reached by other nodes.

**Community Detection**: The process of identifying groups or clusters of nodes that are more densely connected to each other than to the rest of the network.

**Composite Indicator**: An aggregated index formed when individual indicators are compiled into a single index based on an underlying model.

## D

**Degree Centrality**: The simplest centrality metric, measuring the number of direct connections a node has.

## E

**ELECTRE**: A family of multi-criteria decision analysis methods based on outranking relations. Uses concordance and discordance analysis to compare alternatives.

**Emergence**: The phenomenon where complex patterns arise from relatively simple interactions between components. In this framework, emergent behaviors refer to unexpected outcomes from SDG interactions.

## L

**Label Propagation**: A community detection algorithm that assigns labels to nodes based on the labels of their neighbors, iteratively propagating labels through the network.

## M

**MCDA (Multi-Criteria Decision Analysis)**: A sub-discipline of operations research that explicitly considers multiple criteria in decision-making. Provides structured methods for evaluating alternatives based on conflicting objectives.

**Monte Carlo Simulation**: A computational algorithm that relies on repeated random sampling to obtain numerical results. Used in this framework for uncertainty quantification and robustness testing.

## P

**PageRank**: An algorithm used to measure the importance of nodes in a network based on the concept that important nodes are linked to by other important nodes. Originally developed for web search ranking.

**PROMETHEE**: A ranking method in multi-criteria decision analysis that uses preference functions to compare alternatives pairwise.

## R

**Resilience**: The capacity of a system to absorb disturbance and reorganize while undergoing change, so as to still retain essentially the same function, structure, identity, and feedbacks.

**Risk Propagation**: The process by which risks spread through a network, potentially causing cascading failures. In this framework, modeled through dependency graphs and betweenness analysis.

## S

**Synergy**: Combined effects that are greater than the sum of individual effects. In SDG context, positive synergies occur when progress in one goal accelerates progress in another.

**Systemic Risk**: Risk that affects an entire system rather than just individual components. Characterized by correlation, contagion, and cascading effects.

## T

**TOPSIS (Technique for Order Preference by Similarity to Ideal Solution)**: A multi-criteria decision analysis method that ranks alternatives based on their distance to an ideal solution and anti-ideal solution.

**Tradeoff**: A situation where gaining something requires losing something else. In SDG context, tradeoffs occur when progress in one goal impedes progress in another.

## U

**Uncertainty Quantification**: The process of quantifying uncertainty in model inputs and propagating it through to outputs, typically using statistical methods like Monte Carlo simulation.

## W

**Weight Sensitivity**: Analysis of how changes in the weights assigned to different criteria affect the final ranking or decision outcome. Used to assess robustness of MCDA results.
